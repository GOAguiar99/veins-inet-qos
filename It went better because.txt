Mechanism (what changed behavior)

vo_guarded likely uses bounded blocking (shorter block windows / lower continuous blocking cap), so BE is degraded but not starved.
That avoids large BE queue bursts and retransmission cascades that pollute contention and hurt everyone.
VO still keeps first access advantage (AIFS/CW/TXOP), so VO QoS improves while the channel stays more stable.
Why vo_stable looked worse overall

It likely over-blocks BE during VO demand.
This creates rare but huge BE tail events (be_delay_max_ms = 27+ ms), which indicate instability.
Its BE p95 looks fine only because those extreme spikes are rare (so p95 hides them).
Important caveat

You still have 1 run/config and very different be_tx_count across configs (e.g., 38883 vs 62083), so load is not perfectly matched.
Conclusion is directionally strong, but confirm with multiple seeds (at least 5) before locking it in.
Also, your mac_drop_be_count / mac_drop_vo_count are still 0/NaN here, so per-AC attribution likely wasn’t captured in this dataset yet (old fallback path).
